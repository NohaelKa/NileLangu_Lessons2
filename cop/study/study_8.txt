ϧⲉⲗⲗⲟ^§: Old Man

ⲁⲧⲁⲣⲓⲕⲓ^§: Without Blame
Widely used today in Egyptian Arabic but the original grammer is no longer used so the word changes forms according to Arabic.

ϧⲉⲛⲓ^§: Fight

ⲟⲩⲁϩ^§: Oasis
In Egyptian Arabic and Arabic it is "waHa"

ⲟⲩⲁϩⲉⲙ^§: Repeat action
In Egyptian Arabic it has changed to meaning to mean "a woman who is pregnant and has food cravings".
The reason this word was used for that reason, is because the woman would "repeatedly" ask her requests to her family when she is pregnant.

ⲕⲏⲕ^§: Cake
Similar to "kaHk" in Egyptian Arabic. Did you know that Ancient Egyptians started the kaHk tradition? They would draw lines on the kaHk as a representation of the sun god Aten.

ⲁϧ^§: Ouch

ⲓⲟϩ^§: Moon
It was used in an old Egyptian Arabic song for Ramadan called "waHawy ya waHawy".
Did you know that in the Sahidic (aka Se3eedi) dialect of Egyptian Coptic the moon is called "yaeH"? Both "yoH" and "yaeH" make it in the song "waHawy ya waHawy", celebrating the moons cycle.

ⲓϧ^§: Demon
